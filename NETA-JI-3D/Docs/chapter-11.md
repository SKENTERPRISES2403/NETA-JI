# Chapter 11: Janata Ka MLA

## Purpose

Chapter 11 converts Azad's assembly victory into measurable constituency service and legislative duty. The player opens a token-based office, manages a fictional public allocation, files evidence-backed questions, submits committee records, funds education transport, chooses a works-selection process, coordinates flood relief, protects a whistleblower, publishes attendance and votes, and faces an independent citizen review.

## MLA State

- `LEGISLATIVE`: questions, committee evidence, attendance, voting, and policy effectiveness
- `SERVICE`: constituency cases, public access, emergency response, and delivery
- `ETHICS`: disclosures, conflicts, procurement, evidence protection, and audit quality
- `FUND`: remaining fictional constituency public allocation in lakh rupees
- `SCORE`: persisted MLA performance score and term-on-track result
- Save v11 stores Chapter 11 progress and legislative strategy

## Performance Calculation

The review combines Legislative Effectiveness, Constituency Service, Ethics, the earlier Governance Review, Reputation, and an Opposition Pressure penalty. The term remains on track only with a won assembly seat, ethics of at least 60, a score of at least 70, and no negative public-allocation balance.

## Playable Work

- Public oath, conflicts, and attendance disclosure
- Token-based constituency office with separate public funds
- Privacy-safe case dashboard
- Evidence-backed fictional hospital procurement question
- Public-accounts committee evidence submission
- Costed rural student transport and safe-stop allocation
- Open project scorecard versus closed recommendation-list decision
- Non-partisan flood shelters and public relief stock with Samrat
- Protected fictional whistleblower evidence channel
- Machine-readable attendance, questions, votes, and conflicts
- Shanti-led citizen report card with opposition speaking time
- Computed independent MLA performance review

## Safety Rules

- The assembly, funds, officials, contractors, cases, and allegations are fictional
- Real parties, leaders, officeholders, constituencies, and legislative claims are not used
- Police remain professionally neutral and coordinate only emergency safety
- Party, personal, campaign, ward, and MLA public funds remain separate
- Citizen identities are masked in public dashboards and protected disclosures
