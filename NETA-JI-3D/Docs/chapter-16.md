# Chapter 16: CM Ke Pehle 100 Din

## Purpose

Chapter 16 turns the fictional state-election majority into accountable government. Azad verifies the mandate and oath, publishes cabinet and budget records, starts health, learning, safety, grievance and disaster systems, chooses a governance-control model, audits procurement, faces an open hearing, and submits the first hundred days to an independent performance panel.

## Governance State

- `DELIVERY`: measured health, education, safety, grievance and district execution
- `INTEGRITY`: cabinet disclosures, conflicts, procurement, appeals and public scrutiny
- `FISCAL`: costed priorities, reserves, debt limits and spending discipline
- `SCORE`: computed CM governance score and persisted 100-day result
- Save v16 stores Chapter 16 progress, governance approach, score and review result

## Review Calculation

The review combines Delivery, Cabinet Integrity, Fiscal Discipline, the Chapter 14 leadership score, Reputation, Proof, and an Opposition Pressure penalty. Passing requires the Chapter 15 majority, all three governance metrics at 60 or above, and an overall score of at least 75.

## Playable Work

- Independent majority, oath, asset, conflict and administration-separation audit
- Public files and recusal rules for twelve fictional cabinet members
- Open state budget with priority costs, debt limit and contingency reserve
- Essential medicines, maternal referral, ambulance and hospital grievance command
- Teacher support, foundational learning and dropout-outreach baseline
- Shanti-led women, children, caregiver, student and accessibility safety forum
- Open district dashboard versus centralized headline-drive decision
- Receipt-to-appeal grievance tracking across eighteen fictional districts
- Samrat-led neutral heatwave, flood, crowd and missing-child response drill
- Tender, conflict, price, delivery and appeal red-flag audit
- Equal-time public hearing with residents, opposition and press
- Computed independent CM hundred-day review

## Safety Rules

- The state, cabinet, districts, departments, tenders, incidents, claims and outcomes are fictional
- No real party, leader, minister, government, police allegation, caste, religion or identity-targeted policy is used
- Samrat supports neutral public-safety procedure; political staff never direct enforcement
- Public metrics exclude protected identity, private family and victim data
- Political publicity cannot replace delivery evidence, procurement checks or fiscal gates
