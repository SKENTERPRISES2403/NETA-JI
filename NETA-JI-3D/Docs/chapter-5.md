# Chapter 5: Dawa Ka Sach

## Purpose

Chapter 5 completes the family-origin arc that pushes Azad toward systemic reform. Shanti survives an emergency, the unborn child cannot be saved, and the playable response centers on consent, donor coordination, transparent payments, verified records, and a fictional medicine-supply case.

## New Proof Mechanic

- `PROOF` is a persistent fourth stat alongside Trust, Funds, and Reputation.
- Stock records, batch labels, tender copies, and sealed evidence increase Proof.
- Unverified public allegations can reduce Proof even while increasing short-term Trust.
- Future opposition, election, policy, and governance systems can consume or require Proof.

## Objective Flow

1. Support Shanti and arrange emergency transport.
2. Complete triage history and read the consent form.
3. Activate verified Helpers Hand blood donors.
4. Compare pharmacy stock, batch, and invoice records.
5. Receive the private doctor update.
6. Choose a verified case file or immediate public supplier post.
7. Obtain the certified fictional tender copy.
8. Seal the evidence chain with Constable Samrat.
9. Meet Shanti in the recovery garden and commit to system change.

## Decision Outcomes

- Verified case file: Trust 97, Funds Rs 150, Reputation 71, Proof 17, decision 1.
- Immediate public post: Trust 99, Funds Rs 250, Reputation 64, Proof 9, decision 2.
- Both paths keep Shanti stable and preserve the same non-graphic family outcome.

## Safety Contract

- No medical procedure is playable or graphically shown.
- Dialogue avoids blaming an individual doctor without verified evidence.
- The hospital, supplier, tender, records, and allegations are fictional.
- The chapter does not provide medical advice; it portrays documentation and civic accountability.
