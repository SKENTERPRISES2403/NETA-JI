# Chapter 17: Badlav Ke Paanch Saal

## Purpose

Chapter 17 expands the hundred-day government into a full fictional state term. Azad freezes independent baselines, improves primary and maternal care, measures learning recovery, builds livelihoods, reforms safety follow-up, chooses a five-year reform model, delivers rural and urban outcomes, audits integrity, defends the public record, and faces an independent term review.

## Outcome State

- `HEALTH`: primary care, medicine, maternal-child referral and access outcomes
- `LEARNING`: reading, maths, attendance, teacher support and inclusion outcomes
- `SAFETY`: response, evidence, survivor support, appeal and safe-service outcomes
- `LIVELIHOOD`: skills, enterprise survival, fair credit, producer and work outcomes
- `SCORE`: computed full-term state outcome score and persisted review result
- Save v17 stores Chapter 17 progress, reform approach, score and term-review result

## Review Calculation

The term review gives equal weight to Health, Learning, Safety and Livelihood, then adds the CM hundred-day score, Cabinet Integrity, Reputation and Proof, with an Opposition Pressure penalty. Passing requires the hundred-day audit, every outcome at 60 or above, and an overall score of at least 78.

## Safety Rules

- The state, districts, departments, cases, providers, samples and outcomes are fictional
- No real party, leader, government, police allegation, caste, religion or identity-targeted policy is used
- Health and safety reports exclude private medical, survivor and family data
- Police neutrality, due process, appeals and independent sample checks remain explicit
- Registration, spending, arrest, footfall and announcement counts cannot substitute for verified outcomes
