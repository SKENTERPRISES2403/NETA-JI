# Chapter 23: Desh Ka Badlav

## Purpose

Chapter 23 reviews ten years of fictional national development through measurable health, learning, safety-and-justice, and livelihood outcomes. It replaces impossible promises such as eliminating all crime with audited prevention, response, due process, access, and quality indicators. No single headline can compensate for failure in another public outcome.

## National Development State

- `HEALTH`: primary care, maternal safety, medicines, emergency referral, staffing, and complaint response
- `LEARNING`: foundational and adult learning, teachers, libraries, scholarships, disability access, and verified outcomes
- `SAFETY`: prevention, missing-person response, forensics, survivor support, legal aid, case tracking, accountability, and due process
- `LIVELIHOOD`: skills, apprenticeships, enterprise access, payment reliability, worker safety, rural value chains, and migrants
- `SCORE`: computed independent ten-year national development review
- `PASSED`: true only after the Prime Minister 100-day review and all four development gates
- Save v23 stores all four indices, the long-term strategy decision, score, and review result

## Review Calculation

The score combines Health (23%), Learning (22%), Safety and Justice (20%), Livelihood (19%), the PM 100-day score (8%), Institutional Trust (4%), Proof (2%), and a Pressure penalty (4%).

Passing requires the PM 100-day review, all four outcomes at 68 or above, and a total score of at least 82. The mega-target route can improve health and jobs faster, but it reaches only a narrow pass after losing learning depth and justice safeguards.

## Playable Work

- National statistical baseline with privacy, missing-data, sampling, and correction rules
- Universal health-access network and independent coverage samples
- Fictional 95 learning-access index with verified outcomes instead of enrollment-only claims
- Prevention, forensics, survivor support, legal aid, police accountability, and due process
- Livelihood network covering skills, payments, enterprises, worker safety, and migration
- Evidence-led phased reform versus mega-target sprint strategy decision
- Federal finance compact with state flexibility, reserves, consent, audits, and opt-out rules
- Open procurement, ownership, delivery samples, appeals, and whistleblower records
- Water, energy, heat, flood, river, agriculture, and disaster resilience review
- Shanti-led household experience, accessibility, grievance, and independent-media review
- Samrat's neutral field report including unresolved weak districts
- Independent computed ten-year national development audit

## Safety Rules

- Every party, official, programme, district result, vendor, dispute, and allegation remains fictional
- No real leader, party, election result, caste, religion, or targeted identity messaging is used
- Safety reform includes due process, legal aid, survivor support, evidence quality, and accountability
- The game never claims that crime, abuse, or corruption can be reduced to absolute zero
- Samrat remains a neutral constable and reports weaknesses without political control
- States retain finance flexibility, data consent, grievance paths, and opt-out protections
