# Chapter 12: Zila Sangathan

## Purpose

Chapter 12 expands Azad's work from one assembly constituency into an accountable fictional district network. The player maps public issues, vets candidates, trains local leaders, connects rural and urban service desks, chooses a candidate-selection process, audits funding, corrects misinformation, holds a public convention, and faces an independent readiness review.

## District State

- `REACH`: issue desks and coordinators connected across fictional district zones
- `QUALITY`: disclosed service records, public interviews, and candidate standards
- `DISCIPLINE`: funding, consent, complaints, and organization-rule compliance
- `SCORE`: persisted district expansion score and network-ready result
- Save v12 stores Chapter 12 progress and district strategy

## Readiness Calculation

The review combines District Reach, Candidate Quality, Organization Discipline, the earlier MLA Performance score, Reputation, and an Opposition Pressure penalty. Expansion is approved only when the MLA term remains on track, Candidate Quality and Discipline are at least 60, and the computed score is at least 75.

## Playable Work

- Independent MLA-record and expansion-eligibility audit
- Comparable public-issue map for six fictional zones
- Disclosure-based candidate vetting and public interview
- Shanti-led women and youth leadership cohort
- Rural block and urban zone service networks
- Open local primary versus closed appointment decision
- Zone-wise donation and organization-expense audit
- Source-backed correction of a fictional candidate rumour
- Evidence-led district convention with public objections
- Candidate, consent, funding, and anti-hate compliance review
- Computed independent district-network readiness result

## Safety Rules

- District zones, candidates, election claims, allegations, and organizations are fictional
- Real parties, leaders, officeholders, constituencies, and protected-trait voter profiles are not used
- Candidate scoring excludes religion, caste, family identity, rumours, and other protected traits
- Police remain professionally neutral; party and public funds stay separate
- Funding, consent, complaints, conflicts, and appeals are auditable
