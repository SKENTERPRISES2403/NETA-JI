# Chapter 19: Pehla Rashtriya Faisla

## Purpose

Chapter 19 is Azad's first fictional national election. It deliberately avoids a guaranteed win: the player verifies eligibility, publishes a costed manifesto, checks candidates, provides language access, runs an issue yatra, debates, chooses a campaign model, discloses funding, trains polling volunteers, secures the event, respects campaign silence, and then narrowly loses an independent count. Accepting the result and serving as a responsible opposition is the chapter's success state.

## Election State

- `SUPPORT`: national campaign support earned through public work and outreach
- `RULES`: lawful campaign compliance, disclosures, corrections, and silence-period discipline
- `OPS`: polling accessibility, observer conduct, incident records, and result-form readiness
- `VOTE`: computed vote share in a fictional national election
- `SEATS`: computed result in a fictional 100-seat national council
- Save v19 stores Chapter 19 progress, campaign approach, result, and contested/won states

## Result Calculation

Vote share combines Campaign Support, Rules, Polling Operations, prior National Readiness, Federal Alliance Trust, Reputation and Proof, then subtracts Opposition Pressure. Seats are derived from vote share in a fictional 100-seat council. A win would require national readiness, Rules and Operations at 60 or above, at least 50% vote, and a 51-seat majority. Both tested routes earn 49% and 42 seats, so the first mandate is lost and the opposition-service arc begins.

## Playable Work

- Eligibility, regional endorsements, disclosures, and deadlines audit
- Costed health, learning, safety, livelihood, and fiscal manifesto
- Candidate disclosure, conflict, serious-case, interview, and appeal checks
- Shanti-led multilingual, easy-read, sign-supported campaign media
- Small-meeting, listening-card, consent-based ground yatra
- Equal-time public debate with sourced claims and correction window
- Issue-first federation versus spectacle-convoy strategy decision
- Donation source, cap, media invoice, and vendor-conflict audit
- Polling accessibility, queue help, observer conduct, and result-form training
- Samrat-led neutral emergency, evidence, and peaceful-queue safety drill
- Campaign silence, correction, promotion-pause, and result-acceptance protocol
- Independent fictional 100-seat national count

## Safety Rules

- The council, election, candidates, rivals, regions, campaign events, and results are fictional
- No real party, leader, constituency, election result, allegation, caste, religion, or targeted identity messaging is used
- The story makes no rigging or conspiracy claim; Azad accepts a lawful defeat
- Police remain neutral and outside the campaign organization
- Support alone cannot win; rules, operations, readiness, alliance trust, and a majority are required

## Verified Outcomes

- Spectacle Convoy: Trust 100, Funds Rs 0, Reputation 92, Proof 94, Power 77, Team 434, Pressure 100, Support 100, Rules 82, Ops 98, Vote 49%, Seats 42/100, contested true, won false
- Both routes persist campaign approaches 1 and 2, complete the chapter, and preserve the prior 18-region national-readiness result
