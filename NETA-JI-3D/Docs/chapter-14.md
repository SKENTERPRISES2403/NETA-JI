# Chapter 14: Pradesh Ka Netrutva

## Purpose

Chapter 14 turns the six-seat foothold into an accountable fictional state-legislative leadership process. The player verifies the mandate, publishes a caucus ethics charter, builds a costed shadow budget, holds regional hearings, trains a policy council, reviews a neutral emergency plan, chooses a leader-selection process, activates legislative research, corrects misinformation, holds a state policy convention, and faces an independent leadership review.

## Leadership State

- `POLICY`: costed plans, legislative research, emergency readiness, and evidence quality
- `UNITY`: caucus rules, dissent, conflicts, shared process, and internal legitimacy
- `PUBLIC`: public hearings, inclusion, corrections, explanations, and citizen scrutiny
- `SCORE`: computed state leadership score and persisted selection result
- Save v14 stores Chapter 14 progress and leadership strategy

## Selection Calculation

The leadership review combines Policy Credibility, Caucus Unity, Public Leadership, the earlier State Expansion score, Reputation, Proof, and an Opposition Pressure penalty. Selection requires an earned state foothold, Policy and Unity of at least 60, and a score of at least 73.

## Playable Work

- Independent six-seat mandate and leadership-eligibility audit
- Published caucus ethics, dissent, conflict, and whip charter
- Costed shadow budget with funding sources and trade-offs
- Equal-time regional public hearings with privacy-safe records
- Shanti-led women and youth policy leadership council
- Samrat-led neutral flood, heatwave, and crowd-safety plan review
- Open caucus ballot versus closed loyalty deal decision
- Evidence-led legislative research and correction workflow
- Source-backed correction of a fictional leadership rumour
- State policy convention with dissent and unresolved questions
- Assets, endorsements, conflicts, complaints, and vote-count audit
- Computed independent state-leadership selection

## Safety Rules

- State institutions, caucus members, claims, bills, threats, and votes are fictional
- Real parties, leaders, governments, constituencies, and legislative claims are not used
- Police and administration remain neutral; Samrat supports public-safety procedure only
- Protected identity, private family data, retaliation, and identity-based targeting are prohibited
- Policy costs, donors, endorsements, conflicts, dissent, and corrections remain auditable
