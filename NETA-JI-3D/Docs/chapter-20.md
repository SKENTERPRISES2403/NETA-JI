# Chapter 20: Haar Ke Baad Himmat

## Purpose

Chapter 20 follows Azad through five years of responsible opposition after his first national-election defeat. Progress comes from accepting the lawful result, maintaining a public service network, publishing evidence-led policy alternatives, protecting institutional neutrality, renewing regional partnerships, and releasing an honest public report. Defeat is treated as democratic feedback, not a conspiracy or instant game-over state.

## Opposition State

- `SERVICE`: measurable public assistance, legislative attendance, relief work, and accessible learning support
- `ALLIANCE`: renewed regional trust protected by written autonomy, consent, language, and opt-out rules
- `POLICY`: sourced alternatives, corrections, fiscal notes, and evidence-led scrutiny
- `SCORE`: computed five-year comeback review
- `READY`: eligibility for a second fictional national campaign
- Save v20 stores Chapter 20 progress, the opposition strategy decision, all three records, and the comeback review

## Comeback Calculation

The review combines Service (30%), Alliance Renewal (27%), Policy Correction (23%), prior National Readiness (8%), first-election vote (5%), Reputation (3%), Proof (2%), and an Opposition Pressure penalty (5%). A comeback also requires the first election to have been contested and lost, Alliance and Policy at 60 or above, and a total score of at least 74.

## Playable Work

- Peaceful count acceptance, concession, de-escalation, and transition record
- Council attendance, public questions, committee notes, amendments, and recusals
- Paperwork, legal-referral, school, health-navigation, and disaster-help service network
- Costed shadow-policy alternatives with sourced critique and correction notes
- Neutral cross-party relief coordinated around a shared needs list
- Evidence watchdog report with response rights, source protection, and legal review
- Issue-by-issue opposition versus constant-confrontation strategy decision
- Shanti-led public learning, civic forms, scholarship, and digital-safety network
- Samrat-led neutral safety, emergency-referral, and evidence-handover protocol
- Regional autonomy charter covering language, leadership, consent, finance, and opt-out rights
- Five-year public report including promises, service, finance, complaints, and mistakes
- Independent computed comeback review

## Safety Rules

- All councils, rivals, regions, disputes, crises, and election outcomes remain fictional
- No real party, leader, constituency result, allegation, caste, religion, or targeted identity messaging is used
- Azad accepts the first-election result and works within democratic institutions
- Samrat remains institutionally neutral and outside party decisions
- Service is not conditional on party membership, and alliance partners retain written autonomy
- Both strategy routes remain playable, but constant confrontation visibly weakens alliance and policy quality
