# Chapter 21: PM Ka Janadesh

## Purpose

Chapter 21 gives Azad a second fictional national election only after five years of responsible opposition and a passed comeback review. The chapter covers an independently counted campaign, a renewed federal agreement, a costed manifesto, lawful polling operations, and a majority that is earned from the saved political record rather than scripted.

## Second National Election State

- `SUPPORT`: public reach earned through listening, service records, and accessible campaigning
- `RULES`: manifesto costing, finance disclosure, campaign compliance, and federal consent
- `OPS`: neutral polling preparation, safety, accessibility, and evidence handling
- `VOTE`: computed fictional national vote share
- `SEAT`: computed result in the prototype's fictional 100-seat house
- `PM`: true only after the comeback gate, a majority vote, and at least 51 seats
- Save v21 stores all election records, the campaign strategy decision, result, and Prime Minister mandate

## Mandate Calculation

Vote share combines second-campaign Support (14%), Rules (8%), Operations (8%), Comeback Score (8%), Alliance Renewal (5%), Policy Correction (4%), Reputation (2%), Proof (1%), first-election vote (3%), a base of 16, and a Pressure penalty of 6%. Seats are calculated from vote share using `round((vote - 20) * 1.60)`.

The election also requires a passed comeback review, Rules and Operations of at least 60, at least 50% of the fictional vote, and a 51-seat majority. This prevents an automatic victory and keeps both verified routes dependent on the complete saved campaign record.

## Playable Work

- Public eligibility and comeback-record audit
- Honest review of the first national-election defeat
- Costed national manifesto with evidence and correction notes
- Renewed federal coalition contract with regional autonomy and opt-out rules
- Shanti-led accessible campaign and citizen-feedback programme
- Service-vote firewall preventing help from being conditional on political support
- Listening-coalition versus massive-wave campaign decision
- Independent Prime Minister-candidate debate
- Campaign funding disclosure and conflict checks
- Samrat-led neutral polling safety and evidence protocol
- Election-silence and verified-result communication plan
- Independent fictional 100-seat national count

## Safety Rules

- Every party, candidate, constituency, region, dispute, and result is fictional
- No real party, leader, symbol, slogan, allegation, caste, religion, or targeted identity messaging is used
- Coalition partners retain written autonomy, consent, language protections, and exit rights
- Public service is separated from vote requests and remains available to everyone
- Samrat remains institutionally neutral and outside campaign decisions
- The independent count is accepted before the Prime Minister-designate ceremony begins

## Verified Outcomes

- Listening Coalition: Trust 100, Funds Rs 0, Reputation 100, Proof 100, Power 78, Team 534, Pressure 85, Support 76, Rules 98, Operations 76, Vote 55%, Seats 56/100, PM true
- Massive Comeback Wave: Trust 100, Funds Rs 0, Reputation 92, Proof 96, Power 83, Team 530, Pressure 96, Support 88, Rules 64, Operations 64, Vote 52%, Seats 51/100, PM true
- Both routes persist strategy choices 1 and 2, complete all twelve objectives, preserve the earlier 49% / 42-seat defeat, and require the saved Chapter 20 comeback review
