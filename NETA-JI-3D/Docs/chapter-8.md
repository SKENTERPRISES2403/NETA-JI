# Chapter 8: Pehle 100 Din

## Purpose

Chapter 8 converts Azad's first democratic mandate into measurable local governance. The player receives a ring-fenced public budget, publishes a baseline, delivers ward services, chooses a procurement path, coordinates a non-partisan emergency response, holds a jan-sunwai, and receives a computed 100-day review.

## Governance State

- `DELIVERY`: verified service completion
- `INTEGRITY`: procurement, records, and public-audit quality
- `BUDGET`: remaining ward budget in lakh rupees, separate from party or personal money
- `REVIEW`: persisted 100-day score and pass/fail result
- Save v8 stores Chapter 8 progress and the governance approach

## Review Calculation

The review combines service delivery, fiscal integrity, reputation, proof, and opposition pressure. A score of at least 70 passes. Integrity below 60 caps the score at 64, while a negative public budget caps it at 55, so fast delivery cannot hide a weak audit trail or overspending.

## Playable Work

- Public budget handover and citizen-audit baseline
- Water-line pressure test and measured payment
- Free paperwork desk with applicant receipts
- Resident-verified streetlight repairs
- Clinic medicine stock and batch audit
- Non-partisan ward learning room
- Drain-work procurement decision
- Monsoon emergency coordination with Constable Samrat
- Public expenditure dashboard and independent jan-sunwai
- Computed 100-day citizen review

## Governance Safeguards

- Public money and party funds remain separate
- Work orders, measurements, vendors, payments, and remaining balance are disclosed
- No party branding on essential public services
- Samrat's emergency role remains professional and politically neutral
- All officials, contractors, cases, and allegations are fictional
- Opposition residents receive equal speaking time in the jan-sunwai
