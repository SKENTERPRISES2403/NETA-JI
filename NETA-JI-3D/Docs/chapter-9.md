# Chapter 9: Vidhansabha Ki Raah

## Purpose

Chapter 9 expands Azad's accountable ward work into an assembly-constituency organization. The player closes the 100-day audit, visits urban and rural issue camps, publishes candidate disclosures, builds a civic coalition, chooses an outreach strategy, debates a fictional opponent, maps booths without identity profiling, and receives a computed MLA nomination result.

## Assembly State

- `REACH`: constituency-wide issue coverage and public contact
- `UNITY`: cooperation across issue-based civic groups and volunteers
- `READY`: disclosure, training, compliance, and booth preparedness
- `NOM`: persisted independent-panel nomination score
- Save v9 stores Chapter 9 progress, expansion strategy, and nomination result

## Nomination Calculation

The nomination score starts at 20 and combines Reach (32%), Unity (25%), Readiness (25%), the 100-day governance score (10%), Reputation (5%), and an Opposition Pressure penalty (8%). Nomination requires both a score of at least 75 and a passed 100-day public review. A failed Chapter 8 shortcut can recover only through the corrective audit objective; nomination is never scripted.

## Playable Work

- Corrected 100-day audit closure and recovered public balance
- Candidate eligibility, assets, liabilities, and party-compliance desk
- Urban water grievance camp with owners and deadlines
- Verified youth apprenticeship roundtable
- Rural irrigation, insurance, and paperwork camp
- Women-safety route audit led by Shanti with Samrat in a neutral police role
- Issue-based civic coalition with no caste or religion targeting
- Public assets, donations, and measurable-promise disclosure
- Ground jan-sabha versus high-pressure dhol-roadshow decision
- Source-backed debate with a fictional opposition spokesperson
- Privacy-safe booth map and volunteer shift planning
- Computed independent-panel MLA nomination

## Safety Rules

- No real party, leader, candidate, symbol, allegation, or election claim
- No caste, religion, fear, or protected-identity voter profiling
- Police provide lawful public-safety guidance without campaign endorsement
- Public records, party funds, personal money, and ward budget remain separate
- All election training, officials, opponents, cases, and constituency groups are fictional
