# Chapter 22: Pradhan Mantri Ke Pehle 100 Din

## Purpose

Chapter 22 turns the earned Prime Minister mandate into accountable national governance. Azad must complete a constitutional transition, publish cabinet and conflict rules, launch four measurable service missions, preserve federal consent and institutional neutrality, and pass an independent first-100-day review. A national majority does not create an automatic review pass.

## Prime Minister Governance State

- `DELIVERY`: measurable first actions in health, learning, safety, livelihood, and public administration
- `CABINET`: merit criteria, disclosures, recusals, federal representation, and removal rules
- `FISCAL`: costed missions, procurement records, public milestones, and budget discipline
- `INSTITUTIONS`: constitutional limits, federal consent, opposition scrutiny, complaint access, and neutral policing
- `SCORE`: computed independent Prime Minister 100-day review
- `PASSED`: true only after the national mandate and all four minimum governance gates
- Save v22 stores all governance records, the strategy decision, score, and review result

## Review Calculation

The score combines Delivery (30%), Union Cabinet Integrity (23%), National Fiscal Discipline (20%), Institutional Trust (17%), Reputation (4%), Proof (3%), second-election vote share (5%), and a Pressure penalty (5%).

Passing requires the earned Prime Minister mandate, all four governance records at 65 or above, and a total score of at least 75. This keeps a high-speed delivery route playable while exposing its cabinet, fiscal, and institutional cost.

## Playable Work

- Constitutional oath, transition record, coalition agreement, and public asset declaration
- Merit-based Union Cabinet criteria with federal representation and removal rules
- Searchable conflicts, gifts, vendors, procurement, and recusal register
- Public 100-day dashboard with owners, budgets, baselines, evidence, and delay reasons
- Health access mission covering emergency referral, maternal care, medicines, and complaints
- Learning recovery mission covering teachers, scholarships, libraries, and accessibility
- Samrat's neutral field-safety review covering missing persons, evidence, witnesses, and complaints
- Livelihood and paperwork pilots covering apprenticeships, payments, small businesses, and migrants
- Open federal cabinet versus central command blitz strategy decision
- Written federal council compact with finance, data consent, grievances, and opt-out rules
- Shanti-led accessible citizen review and formal opposition briefing
- Independent computed national 100-day governance audit

## Safety Rules

- Every party, minister, state delegate, vendor, case, programme result, and opponent remains fictional
- No real party, leader, allegation, caste, religion, election result, or targeted identity messaging is used
- The national flag is not used as a party symbol
- Samrat remains a constable and institutionally neutral; he receives no political promotion or campaign role
- States retain written consent, data, finance, grievance, and opt-out protections
- Opposition questions, public complaints, correction records, and independent audit remain binding after victory
