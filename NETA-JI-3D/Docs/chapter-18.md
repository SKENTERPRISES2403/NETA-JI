# Chapter 18: Desh Bhar Ka Saath

## Purpose

Chapter 18 moves Azad from one successful fictional state into an accountable national federation. The chapter does not claim India, imitate a real party, or treat publicity as political legitimacy. It asks the player to verify eligibility, write federal rules, listen across fictional regions, protect candidate ethics, train volunteers, provide language access, choose an expansion model, publish evidence-led policy, disclose funding, secure the convention, and pass an independent readiness review.

## Outcome State

- `REACH`: trained organization presence across fictional regions
- `ALLIANCE`: trust earned from autonomous regional partners
- `POLICY`: national policy credibility backed by regional evidence
- `SCORE`: computed national readiness score
- `REGIONS`: fictional regional chapters aligned through the review
- Save v18 stores Chapter 18 progress, expansion approach, score, aligned regions, and readiness result

## Readiness Calculation

The review weights organization Reach at 30%, Alliance Trust at 28%, Policy Credibility at 26%, the five-year State Term score at 8%, Reputation at 3%, and Proof at 2%, then subtracts 4% of Opposition Pressure. Passing requires the state-term review, Alliance and Policy at 60 or above, a score of at least 76, and fourteen or more aligned fictional regions.

## Playable Work

- Independent eligibility audit using the prior five-year public record
- Federal constitution for regional autonomy, membership, elections, removal, appeals, and audits
- Eighteen-region listening network with local priorities and language access
- Candidate disclosure, conflict, interview, serious-case, and removal standards
- Volunteer fact-check, consent, crowd-safety, and grievance training
- Shanti-led multilingual and accessible participation charter
- Patient federation versus centralized headline-wave decision
- Evidence-led health, learning, safety, livelihood, and fiscal policy federation
- Donation caps, source checks, public expenses, vendor conflicts, and correction trail
- Samrat-led convention entry, emergency, missing-person, evidence, and peaceful-dissent safety drill
- Open convention with regional votes, dissent notes, amendments, and elected coordination roles
- Computed independent national readiness review

## Safety Rules

- All regional chapters, candidates, councils, policies, votes, organizations, and convention events are fictional
- No real party, leader, election claim, government allegation, caste, religion, or identity-targeted messaging is used
- The Indian national flag is not used as a party symbol
- Regional autonomy, peaceful dissent, due process, public disclosures, and correction records remain explicit
- Reach alone cannot pass; alliance trust, policy credibility, state performance, and independent review are mandatory
